Equity & the Rule of 2-and-4

What your hand is worth

LO1 · estimating equity

Equity is your hand’s chance of winning at showdown, given the cards on the board right now. If you’re holding a flush draw, your equity is really one question: how often does a card you need show up?

You can compute it exactly with the addition law of probability — the chance of hitting on the turn, plus the chance on the river, minus the small overlap:

P(hit) = P(turn) + P(river) − P(both)
= 9/47 + 9/46 − … ≈ 35%

That’s exact — and slow. The Rule of 2-and-4 is the shorthand: multiply your outs by 2 with one street to come, or by 4 with two. It works because a 52-card deck is almost exactly half of 100.

So with 9 outs and two streets: 9 × 4 ≈ 36%. Close to the exact answer, in your head, at the table.
